Have you ever spent hours doomscrolling or watching long gaming videos, and then realized you learned absolutely nothing from all that time?

That is the problem we want to solve.

Today, many people want to learn a new language, but they do not have enough extra time to study consistently. After school, work, and daily responsibilities, language learning can feel like one more task added to an already busy day.

At the same time, people are already spending their free time on YouTube, entertainment, and online content.

So we asked a simple question:

What if even a small part of that leisure time could become meaningful learning time?

That is why we created Lingo Stream.

Lingo Stream is a planned Chrome extension that turns YouTube videos into a lightweight language learning experience through subtitle-based immersion.

Instead of forcing users to pause videos and search for words one by one, our extension keeps the original subtitle visible and adds translations in parentheses after selected words.

For example, a subtitle would still show the original sentence, but around 5 to 15 percent of meaningful words would be followed by a translation in the user’s target language, such as Japanese.

This allows users to keep watching naturally while learning vocabulary in context.

In our planned workflow, the user opens a YouTube video with captions enabled, then opens the extension popup. From there, they choose Auto as the translation provider, select their target language, set the Mix Percent to something like 15 percent for a good balance between readability and vocabulary exposure, and turn on features like Enable and Save Words. Then they click Save and Sync.

Once configured, the extension detects subtitle updates in real time. It listens for caption changes, identifies useful words, filters out low-value words, and inserts translations directly into the subtitle line.

This means users can continue enjoying their videos without losing focus, while still being exposed to new vocabulary naturally.

We also plan to keep subtitle updates responsive even when the viewer skips or scrubs through the timeline, so the experience stays smooth and uninterrupted.

To make learning more effective over time, Lingo Stream also includes a vocabulary management feature.

Saved words are stored locally and displayed in a table with both the original word and its translation. Users can search and filter their saved vocabulary, export it as a CSV file, import previous vocabulary lists, and reset local records when needed.

This turns casual video watching into a growing personal vocabulary bank.

Finally, we plan to include Lingo Quiz, a built-in review mode that turns passive exposure into active recall.

The quiz uses recently saved vocabulary and presents matching rounds with five word pairs at a time. It tracks score, accuracy, remaining words, and overall progress.

This helps users do more than just notice new words — it helps them remember them.

In summary, we created Lingo Stream because people should be able to learn a language during the free time they already have.

Instead of getting nothing from hours of scrolling or passive watching, users can build vocabulary while enjoying the content they already love.

Lingo Stream is our solution for turning entertainment time into language learning time.
