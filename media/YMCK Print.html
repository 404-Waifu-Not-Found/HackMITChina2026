Inspiration

Language learners spend hours watching YouTube with subtitles — yet those hours remain entirely passive.

We asked: what if the subtitles themselves could teach you? Replace a handful of words inline, and the viewer absorbs vocabulary through context — the same mechanism behind natural language acquisition.

Ambient UX meets spaced-exposure pedagogy — learning that happens while you watch.

No flashcard decks, no separate apps, no extra effort. Just press play and let your subtitles do the teaching. The result is a frictionless bridge between entertainment and education.

Architecture

A Caption Observer watches YouTube's subtitle DOM and feeds each new segment to the Processor, which tokenises text, strips stopwords, and picks candidate words at the user's chosen rate.

Candidates hit the Translation Bridge — up to five providers with auto-fallback and local caching. Translated tokens replace the originals inline, and every swap is saved to Vocabulary Storage for quiz review or CSV export.

The Background Service Worker coordinates messaging between the content script and the popup UI, managing state persistence across tab navigations and browser restarts via Chrome's storage API.

The Science — Why Ambient Learning Works

Linguist Stephen Krashen's Input Hypothesis shows that language is acquired when learners encounter comprehensible input — material that is mostly understood, with a small stretch of new vocabulary in context. Lingo Stream operationalises this at zero extra effort: the surrounding sentence provides meaning, making each translated word instantly memorable without interrupting the viewing experience.
